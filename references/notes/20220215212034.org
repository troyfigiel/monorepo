#+title: Exponential family

The single-parameter exponential family is the family of distributions that can be written as

\[
f_X(\mathbf{x} | \mathbf{\theta}) = h(\mathbf{x}) \exp \left[ \sum\limits_{i = 1}^{s} \eta_{i}(\mathbf{\theta}) \cdot T_i(\mathbf{x}) - A(\mathbf{\theta}) \right]
\]

The vector \( \mathbf{T}(\mathbf{x}) \) is a [[id:89bfa238-7a69-4154-86c8-2da95acf095b][sufficient statistic for the exponential family]].

Many well-known distributions are examples of the exponential family: [[id:a59560f9-96e6-4036-83be-843bda2a18cd][!Exponential family]].

#+title: Memoryless

A probability distribution is called memoryless if the following property holds:

\[ \mathrm{Pr}(X > t + s | X \geq t) = \mathrm{Pr}(X > s) \]

Here the variables \( s, t \) can either be discrete or continuous. Only [[id:fd9e0e3d-4ad1-4e43-8794-4e6d6051705d][few distributions are memoryless]].

#+title: Survival analysis

In survival analysis we are modelling a [[id:95c6de89-5f84-4a0e-b1c2-31774df7e699][Non-negative probability distribution]]. A key feature of
survival analysis is dealing with [[id:a3071245-db74-4b50-993a-bd85c1fecbd0][Censoring]].

This means that the standard method of fitting models based on the [[id:7186f362-2b90-4438-bf99-dcce57121834][Maximum likelihood estimate]],
does not work anymore.

Instead of the standard cumulative probability distributions, in survival analysis often the
[[id:9cc23a42-b037-4960-b113-74766502676b][Survival function]] and the [[id:fc020c1e-4677-4310-9775-492fcdf9b0f1][Hazard function]] are used.

Some well-known models that are used in survival analysis are:
- The [[id:64b4e447-e7d9-48f0-b02a-40c0fdd580a7][Kaplan-Meier estimator]]
- The [[id:4b4a04e8-a6f4-47c4-afbd-b501cb1ebb35][Exponential distribution]] or more generally the [[id:e3aca83f-f08d-4d0f-a767-d04105002235][Weibull distribution]]
- The [[id:9be9c9cf-9cf6-45c7-90ac-3cc777a6271d][Cox proportional hazards model]]

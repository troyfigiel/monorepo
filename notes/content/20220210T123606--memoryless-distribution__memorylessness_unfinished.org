#+title:      Memoryless distribution
#+date:       [2022-02-10 Thu 12:36]
#+filetags:   :memorylessness:unfinished:
#+identifier: 20220210T123606

A probability distribution is called memoryless if the following property holds:

\[ \mathrm{Pr}(X > t + s | X \geq t) = \mathrm{Pr}(X > s) \]

Here the variables \( s, t \) can either be discrete or continuous. Only [[denote:20220210T202139][few distributions are memoryless]].

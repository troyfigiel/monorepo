#+title: ?Renormalizing the Lotka-Volterra equations
#+filetags: :appendix:stub:

Consider

\begin{equation*}
\begin{align}
\dot{x} &= ax - \epsilon_1 x y \\
\dot{y} &= -by + \epsilon_2 x y
\end{align}
\end{equation*}

These are the Lotka-Volterra equations when we perturb in the interactions terms between \( x \)
and \( y \).

The fixed points of the equations are given by \( (x, y) = (0, 0) \) and \( (x, y) = (b/\epsilon_2,
a/\epsilon_1) \).

We will study the behaviour around the non-trivial fixed point. For this, we rescale

\begin{equation*}
\begin{align}
x &= \frac{b + \epsilon_1 \xi}{\epsilon_2} \\
y &= \frac{a}{\epsilon_1} + \eta
\end{align}
\end{equation*}

This places the non-trivial fixed point at \( (\xi, \eta) = (0, 0) \).

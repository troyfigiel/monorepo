#+title: ?Direct proof that the harmonic numbers are never integer
#+filetags: :appendix:stub:

# TODO: Write out the proof
Let

\[
H_n = \sum\limits_{m=1}^n \frac{1}{m}
\]

Clearly, \( H_1 = 1 \) is an integer, but \( H_2 = \frac{3}{2} \) is already not an integer and no
other harmonic numbers besides \( H_1 \) are integer.

#+title: Bias-variance tradeoff
#+filetags: :stub:

The bias-variance tradeoff is the idea that the variance of an estimated parameter is in tension
with the bias in the estimated parameter.

This means that [[id:a35d6ca7-f60c-4a3e-8bd0-8b61e271c1de][Higher model complexity decreases bias, but increases variance]].
In other words, we need to find a balance between [[id:b624f740-dffa-4a36-ad26-43f41f2bf387][Overfitting]] and [[id:1ce4a7fb-76aa-4b3c-b3e3-031b9f4b41bb][Underfitting]].

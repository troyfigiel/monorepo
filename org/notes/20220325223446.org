#+title: Stable distribution
#+filetags: :stub:

A distribution is stable if a linear combination of two independent random variables with this
distribution has the same distribution up to location and scaling.

This defines a four-parameter family of distributions with characteristic function

\begin{equation*}
\varphi(t; \alpha, \beta, c, \mu) = \exp \left( it \mu - |ct|^{\alpha} (1 - i \beta \text{sgn}(t) \Phi) \right)
\end{equation*}

where

\begin{equation*}
\Phi =
\begin{cases}
\tan \left( \frac{\pi \alpha}{2} \right) & \alpha \neq 1 \\
-\frac{2}{\pi} \log |ct| & \alpha = 1
\end{cases}
\end{equation*}

Examples are:
- [[id:b0d3cec8-6efb-48d9-9087-3587954884d7][Cauchy distribution]] for \( (\alpha, \beta, c, \mu) = (1, 0, 1, 0) \)
- [[id:4ec68f86-9801-4464-9114-5c9a2704cc42][LÃ©vy distribution]] for \( (\alpha, \beta, c, \mu) = (1/2, 1, 1, 0) \)
- [[id:d59f2d74-dbe3-46e3-8a5e-9849350d24bc][Normal distribution]] for \( (\alpha, \beta, c, \mu) = (2, 0, 1, 0) \)

The stable distribution plays an important role in the [[id:794e2f29-5e05-496f-bbb6-080781da9287][Generalized central limit theorem]].

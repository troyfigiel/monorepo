#+title: Argument principle
#+filetags: :stub:

Consider the problem of counting the difference in some contour \( \mathcal{C} \) between the
number of zeros \( Z(f, \mathcal{C}) \) and the number of poles \( P(f, \mathcal{C}) \) of a
meromorphic function \( f(z) \).

The argument principle relates this number to the logarithmic derivative of \( f(z) \) through

\[ \frac{1}{2 \pi i} \oint\limits_{\mathcal{C}} \frac{f^{\prime}(z)}{f(z)} = Z(f, \mathcal{C}) -
P(f, \mathcal{C}) \]

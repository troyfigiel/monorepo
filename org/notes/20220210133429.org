#+title: Generalized mean

The generalized mean, also known as HÃ¶lder mean or power mean, is defined as

\[ M_p (x_1, ..., x_n) = \left( \sum_{i = 1}^n x_i^p \right)^{1 / p} \]

For some special values, this leads to

- [[id:78007b4e-3486-4df8-a3d3-3df9a171874f][Maximum]] \( p \rightarrow \infty \)
- [[id:3b492b25-7d6f-4283-8899-1f0a201cb7f6][Cubic mean]] \( p = 3 \)
- [[id:72679659-f19d-44b1-8d30-b697aca3cc53][Root mean square]] \( p = 2 \)
- [[id:de880bd6-4759-41c6-84d5-9baeccd85fb1][Arithmetic mean]] \( p = 1 \)
- [[id:2ba59e71-71d2-45ed-b543-dd701757bde0][Geometric mean]] \( p \rightarrow 0 \)
- [[id:0a8c9c6d-3089-4f1f-86c0-8c05e298172e][Harmonic mean]] \( p = -1 \)
- [[id:e0c25fbd-c8d1-48f1-a3d0-61be575c77ce][Minimum]] \( p \rightarrow - \infty \)

Moreover, using [[id:9db117f9-aa26-46d5-9a88-61f29a8b5bc0][Jensen's inequality]] we may prove that

\[ M_p (x_1, ..., x_n) \leq M_q (x_1, ..., x_n) \]

when \( p < q \) with strict equality only for \( x_1 = x_2 = ... = x_n \).

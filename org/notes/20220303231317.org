#+title: Factorial moment generating function
#+filetags: :stub:

The factorial moment generating function of a distribution is defined as \( M_X(t) =
\mathbb{E}[t^X] \).

Since [[id:66f657e7-d56d-4f3c-98a9-4bdd93e68c27][Every probability distribution has a characteristic function]], we know that the moment
generating function is defined at least for \( |t| = 1 \).

For various calculations of generating functions, see: [[id:1531721d-0cd3-46c5-bc67-2988a3ad818a][?Factorial moment generating function]].

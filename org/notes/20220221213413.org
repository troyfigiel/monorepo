#+title: False discovery rate
#+filetags: :stub:

The false discovery rate (FDR) is a conceptualization of the false positive rate when conducting
[[id:8f0c6651-b460-4338-9ad3-d6f2d7de3705][multiple comparisons]].

These procedures have less strict control over false positives compared to [[id:d221a181-8ae8-40d0-836f-844b5185f8c9][familywise error rate]]
controlling procedures.

One example of a method is the [[id:61d0fc1c-8524-4692-a8d1-3ca55aecddbf][Benjamini-Hochberg method]].

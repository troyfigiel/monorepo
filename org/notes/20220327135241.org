#+title: Measure-theoretic basis of probability theory
#+filetags: :stub:

- [[id:e54d54dc-db36-4c3c-a12b-10a6bea4dd30][Measure theory]]
- Unification of [[id:ebbac5a1-47e3-4112-bd8a-a1511f50add0][Discrete probability distribution]] and [[id:8599863e-13c6-45db-8239-6d50e4230975][Continuous probability distribution]]
- This avoids [[id:c2b6da93-961a-4db5-b3ea-96fbe3530635][Bertrand's paradox]] because you specify a measure to integrate against instead of
  assuming one
- It also allows us to describe more exotic distributions that are neither discrete nor continuous.
  ([[id:02b279c8-2829-449c-92be-c5f77a465a16][Zero-inflated model]])
- And more exotic distributions such as the [[id:0bc7a71f-2acc-4131-a38d-5275078cd7cd][Cantor distribution]], which showcases the power of the
  measure-theoretic approach.

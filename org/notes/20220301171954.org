#+title: The sign test is a special case of the binomial test
#+filetags: :stub:

The [[id:0b78d733-bf21-4813-b4c9-c4c155060db6][Sign test]] is a special case of the [[id:279bd970-5d55-425e-bef2-fe55539164b5][Binomial test]].

For a given sample \( \left\{ (x_1, y_1), ..., (x_n, y_n) \right\} \), we remove the pairs \( (x_i,
y_i) \) where \( x_i = y_i \). This leaves us with \( m \) pairs, so that the number of pairs \( 0
\leq W \leq m \) where \( x_j > y_j \) should be distributed according to the [[id:e12885a7-945e-4c5b-92ea-d90f1d750a90][Binomial distribution]]
as \( W \sim \mathrm{Bin}(m, 0.5) \).

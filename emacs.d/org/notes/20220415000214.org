#+title: Hamilton-Jacobi equation
#+filetags: :stub:

This equation describes an alternative formulation of [[id:3ae00f4c-90a2-47d7-b211-b9dc126ad334][Classical mechanics]]. It can be understood as
a special case of the [[id:7cc2beed-fc0e-45a1-ac37-699d94be11dd][Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman equation]].

\[
-\frac{\partial S}{\partial t} = H \left( \mathbf{q}, \frac{\partial S}{\partial \mathbf{q}}, t \right)
\]

The aim of this formulation of [[id:3ae00f4c-90a2-47d7-b211-b9dc126ad334][Classical mechanics]] is to find a generating function for a [[id:02f213c2-c8c2-4d4f-9551-b956100cb09e][Canonical
transformation]] such that the transformed [[id:a00e92f2-6cae-4515-bad8-264c6bc9dc55][Hamiltonian]] (also called the [[id:9b6d9563-61ed-44e9-b229-66b66a75cfe8][Kamiltonian]]) is identically
zero.

This formulation of [[id:3ae00f4c-90a2-47d7-b211-b9dc126ad334][Classical mechanics]] is especially interesting for two reasons:
1. The generating function ([[id:4cdbc355-3e13-404d-9cb3-81f37e78bee3][Hamilton's principal function]]) can be identified with the classical
   [[id:e54f8d35-b5e0-4fe3-b3a4-f259d63cbc18][Action]].
2. This formulation treats waves and particles on equal footing and it is this equation that is
   recovered from the [[id:34f3f3ad-3ded-4885-9f5e-79e94e37b77a][Classical limit]] of the [[id:8fd38676-1174-402c-bbf4-581ec5b5fc56][Schr√∂dinger equation]].

If the [[id:a00e92f2-6cae-4515-bad8-264c6bc9dc55][Hamiltonian]] is time-independent, we can express a solution \( S \) in terms of [[id:9ed31775-37ca-419a-af0f-a6855ff9a298][Hamilton's
characteristic function]] \( W(\mathbf{q}) \) as \( S(\mathbf{q}, t) = W(\mathbf{q}) - E t \) where

\[
H \left( \mathbf{q}, \frac{\partial S}{\partial \mathbf{q}} \right) = E
\]

#+title: few distributions are memoryless

The only memoryless distributions are
- [[id:3ff4bad6-99ea-47ce-8e71-a805878b7372][geometric distribution]]
- [[id:4b4a04e8-a6f4-47c4-afbd-b501cb1ebb35][exponential distribution]]

By definition of conditional probability, we have

\[ \mathrm{Pr(X > t + s)} = \mathrm{Pr(X > t + s | X > t)} \mathrm{Pr(X > t)} \]

By definition of memorylessness, we have

\[ \mathrm{Pr(X > t + s | X > t)} = \mathrm{Pr(X > s)} \]

Rewriting these two equations in terms of the [[id:9cc23a42-b037-4960-b113-74766502676b][survival function]], we find

\( S(t + s) = S(t)S(s) \)

This equation allows us to prove that for any rational number \( t \) we have \( S(t) = S(1)^t \).

In the discrete case this reduces to the [[id:3ff4bad6-99ea-47ce-8e71-a805878b7372][geometric distribution]] whereas in the continous case, the only continous function satisfying the above equation for all rational \( t \) is the [[id:4b4a04e8-a6f4-47c4-afbd-b501cb1ebb35][exponential distribution]].

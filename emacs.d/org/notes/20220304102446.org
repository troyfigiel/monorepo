#+title: ?Factorial moments of the Poisson distribution
#+filetags: :appendix:stub:

With \( \mathbb{P}(X=k) = e^{- \lambda} \frac{\lambda^k}{k!} \) we find

\begin{equation*}
\begin{align}
M_X(t)
&= e^{- \lambda} \sum\limits_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{(t \lambda)^k}{k!} \\
&= e^{\lambda (t - 1)}
\end{align}
\end{equation*}

Hence, \( \mathbb{E}[(X)_r] = \lambda^r \).
